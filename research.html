Research

What I work on 🧠

My research focuses on how complex systems learn, make decisions, and anticipate future events under uncertainty. I combine large-scale neural data analysis, computational modeling, and behavioral experiments to study how distributed brain networks coordinate goal-directed behavior. Using tools from information theory, reinforcement learning, and statistical modeling, I investigate how biological systems process information, adapt to changing environments, and optimize actions. Beyond basic neuroscience, this work connects to broader questions in artificial intelligence, adaptive systems, and data-driven modeling of decision processes.

Motor Prediction and Fronto-Parietal Network Dynamics

I studied how premotor and posterior parietal areas support anticipatory movements. Using large-scale simultaneous neural recordings, dynamical systems and advanced statistical analysis, I examined how the brain anticipates future actions when environmental structure becomes predictable.

Decision-Making Under Social Uncertainty in Premotor Circuits

I investigated how premotor circuits represent uncertainty during social interaction. Using information-theoretic analyses of neural population activity, I studied how cooperative decision strategies emerge when agents must coordinate actions to maximize reward.

Information Gain and Learning in Fronto-Cingulate Networks

I examined how the brain evaluates information during trial-and-error learning. By combining reinforcement learning models with behavioral and neural data, I investigated how frontal and cingulate regions encode information gain during trial-and-error decision making.
